
# Hyderabad Zomato Restaurant Analysis 🍽️

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a Hyderabad Zomato dataset sourced from Kaggle.
Built as part of my M.Sc. Data Science portfolio (2025).

## 📊 What This Project Covers

- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Univariate Analysis — ratings distribution, price distribution, top cuisines
- Bivariate Analysis — price vs rating, cuisine vs average rating, cuisine vs average price
- Correlation heatmap between price and ratings
- Business insights and conclusions

## ❓ Key Questions Answered

- Are expensive restaurants actually rated better in Hyderabad?
- Which cuisines are most popular vs most highly rated?
- What is the most common price range for dining in Hyderabad?
- Is there a correlation between price and rating?

## 📌 Dataset

- **Source:** Kaggle
- **Name:** Hyderabad Zomato Dataset
- **Columns used:** Name, Cuisine, Ratings, Price for One, Link

## 💡 Key Findings

- Most restaurants in Hyderabad fall in the ₹200–₹400 price range for one person
- Price and rating show weak/no correlation — expensive does not mean better rated
- There is a noticeable scope for improvement as most restaurants are ranked 3.5-4 according to the data
- Some cuisines offer high ratings at relatively lower prices, indicating strong value-for-money segments.
